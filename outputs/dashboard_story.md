# Dashboard Story — Executive Sales Dashboard
## Retail Performance Overview 2024–2025
### Written for Leadership Audience

---

## Executive Summary

Across 4,200 orders and Rs 21.70 Crore in total sales during 2024–2025, the retail business is fundamentally sound — every region is profitable, three customer segments are well-balanced, and Technology is a high-performing engine delivering 18.22% margins. However, two areas demand leadership attention before the next planning cycle: **Furniture's combined low-margin and high-return profile**, and **a discounting practice that produces loss-making orders above the 30% threshold**. The dashboard, built across 11 sheets with 4 KPI cards and 7 analytical views, provides the evidence for both the strengths to protect and the risks to address.

## What Is Performing Well

### Technology Is Carrying the Business

The Category Profitability chart makes this impossible to miss: Technology's bar stands at Rs 2.80 Cr in profit, towering above Furniture (Rs 35.6L) and Office Supplies (Rs 17.1L). With an 18.22% margin and only a 3.03% return rate, Technology is the most efficient category in the portfolio. Sub-categories tell a consistent story — Copiers (Rs 73.1L profit), Accessories (Rs 71.9L), Phones (Rs 71.0L), and Machines (Rs 64.5L) all perform strongly. No Technology sub-category is loss-making.

**Story link:** The Sales Trend chart confirms that Technology drives the monthly revenue line (orange). The Discount vs Profit scatter shows Technology data points (one colour) remaining profitable at moderate discount levels — validating that Technology can absorb some discounting without destroying value.

### South Region Leads; All Regions Are Profitable

The Regional Performance chart uses colour (from red to green, encoded by Profit) to show that all four regions sit in profitable territory — no red bars. South is the clear volume leader at Rs 6.47 Cr, with North second (Rs 5.46 Cr). The colour coding also reveals East as having the strongest margin intensity despite lower absolute sales, and West as slightly cooler (lower margin) compared to East.

**Story link:** When the dashboard filter is applied to "South" (as shown in the filter_interaction_view screenshot), all seven charts simultaneously update to show South-only performance — this is the dashboard's filter action working correctly.

### Customer Segments Are Well-Balanced

The Customer Segment View shows three nearly equal segments — Home Office (Rs 7.45 Cr), Consumer (Rs 7.19 Cr), and Corporate (Rs 7.06 Cr) — with profit colour encoding showing broadly similar profitability. No single segment dominates, which reduces concentration risk. If one segment contracts, the other two provide a buffer.

## What Is Underperforming

### Furniture: The Double Risk

Furniture appears in two different charts telling the same story from different angles. In the Category Profitability chart, Furniture's stacked bar is well below the Average reference line — its profit (Rs 35.6L) is less than half the portfolio average. In the Return Analysis chart, Furniture has the tallest stacked bar across all three categories, with Home Office segment (red) contributing the largest single block of returns (37 returns out of Furniture's 88 total).

This is a double-risk profile: thin margins AND high returns. Every returned Furniture order was already low-margin to begin with, and the return adds reverse logistics cost on top.

**Story link:** The Discount vs Profit scatter plot adds a third data point — Furniture points at high discount levels dip furthest below the zero-profit line, meaning Furniture has the least resilience to discounting of any category.

### West Region Leaves Margin on the Table

Despite matching East's sales figure almost exactly (Rs 4.89 Cr each), West generates a lower margin (15.14% vs East's 15.55%). Over a full year of Rs 21.7 Cr in total sales, a 0.4 percentage point margin gap is material. The Regional Performance chart's colour encoding visually shows West as a slightly cooler shade than East, confirming this.

## What Risks Are Visible

### 1. The 30%+ Discount Loss Zone

The Discount vs Profit scatter plot is the most analytically important chart on the dashboard. The downward trend lines for all three categories converge at zero profit around 25–30% discount. At the 31–50% discount band, average profit per order is Rs −1,601 — structurally loss-making. Leadership can see this directly from the chart: points scattered at 0.30+ on the X axis cluster below the zero line on the Y axis.

### 2. August Revenue Dip — Recurring and Predictable

The Sales Trend line chart shows a visible dip every August. In 2024, August was Rs 63.0L — the lowest month of the year. In 2025, August recovered to Rs 1.09 Cr, but still shows a local trough relative to surrounding months. The dip affects both the orange (Sales) and blue (Profit) lines simultaneously.

### 3. Standard Class Delivery at Scale

The Shipping Performance chart shows the "8+ Days" and "4–7 Days" delay buckets carrying the heaviest bars. Standard Class (4.71-day average) handles 58% of all orders. If this speed correlates with lower customer satisfaction or higher return rates, the concentration in Standard Class creates an amplified risk at scale.

### 4. Furniture × Home Office Return Concentration

The Return Analysis chart makes clear that the Home Office segment (red stacked portion) contributes the most Furniture returns (37 of 88). This specific combination — Home Office customers buying Furniture — is the single highest-risk order type in the portfolio.

## What Opportunities Are Visible

### 1. Technology Can Grow Further

With 18.22% margin, 3.03% return rate, and four strong sub-categories, Technology has room to grow. Increasing marketing investment specifically for Technology (Copiers, Accessories) in West and North regions — where Technology's margin premium could lift overall regional performance — is the clearest growth lever.

### 2. East Region's Margin Discipline Is Exportable

East achieves the highest margin (15.55%) despite mid-table sales volume. Its pricing and discount behaviour is more disciplined than West. If leadership can identify what East does differently — lower discount approvals, better customer mix, higher-margin product emphasis — that playbook can be replicated in West to close the 0.4pp gap.

### 3. Reducing Furniture Returns Would Unlock Immediate Margin

If the 88 Furniture returns could be halved (to ~44), Furniture's return rate would drop from 7.67% to ~3.8%, approaching the Office Supplies rate. Fewer returns means fewer reverse logistics costs, and each retained sale contributes to Furniture's thin but positive 6.89% margin. This is a high-leverage operational improvement.

## Limitations of This Dashboard

1. **Profit Margin KPI formula error:** The Profit Margin KPI card currently displays 55,118.9% instead of 15.35%. This is a Tableau aggregation issue where individual row margins are summed rather than total profit divided by total sales. This must be corrected before sharing with leadership.

2. **Profit is gross profit only:** The profit field is sales minus cost of goods. It does not include operating expenses, shipping costs, or return handling costs. The true net margin would be lower than 15.35%.

3. **Return reason not captured:** The dataset flags returns (1/0) but does not record why the item was returned. "Not as described," "damaged in transit," and "changed mind" require different interventions but cannot be distinguished from this data.

4. **No customer lifetime value:** The dashboard is order-level. A customer who places 10 small orders appears the same as one placing 10 large orders in aggregate metrics. High-value repeat customers cannot be identified.

5. **No forecasting:** The Sales Trend chart shows history. It does not predict future months. A separate forecasting model would be needed to project the August 2026 dip in advance.

6. **Shipping Performance measures delivery days, not customer experience:** Delivery days is a proxy for satisfaction. Whether customers actually care about the delay depends on their expectations — this correlation is not in the current dataset.

## Suggested Next Analysis

1. **Fix Profit Margin KPI** — Change formula to `{SUM([profit])/SUM([sales])}` in Tableau and reformat as percentage.
2. **Furniture sub-category deep dive** — Which specific sub-categories are profit positive vs negative? Tables and Bookcases are the key suspects.
3. **Discount approval audit** — Which regions, segments, and product categories have the highest proportion of 25%+ discount orders?
4. **Delivery days vs customer rating regression** — Is slower delivery statistically correlated with lower customer ratings in this dataset?
5. **State-level drill-down within South** — Which states drive South's volume leadership, and are any showing signs of margin erosion?
